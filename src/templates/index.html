    Volunteer eyes and ears

    Eighty miles north of the Texas-Mexico border in Brooks County, South Texan rancher Mike Vickers leads the Texas Border Volunteers, a group of nearly 300 men and women who gather monthly to conduct undercover operations along the border. Their mission is to report criminal trespassing by undocumented immigrants crossing the border into the United States. Some think they are just vigilantes, but multiple law enforcement agencies across South Texas rely on them as an extra set of eyes and ears to help the Border Patrol.
